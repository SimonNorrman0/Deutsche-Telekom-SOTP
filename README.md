# Deutsche Telekom AG Valuation

## Overview

This project presents a full valuation of Deutsche Telekom AG using a **Sum-of-the-Parts (SOTP)** framework.

Due to Deutsche Telekom's diversified business structure and majority ownership of **T-Mobile US (TMUS)**, each operating segment was valued separately and combined to derive an implied equity value and target share price.

The analysis includes:

- Segment-level DCF valuations
- Sum-of-the-Parts (SOTP) valuation
- Trading Comparable Company Analysis
- Precedent Transaction Analysis
- Football Field Valuation
- Very brief Investment Memo

---

## Investment Thesis

The market values Deutsche Telekom primarily through a European telecom perspective while underappreciating the value of its majority stake in T-Mobile US.

A Sum-of-the-Parts approach reveals that the standalone value of the TMUS stake, combined with Deutsche Telekom's highly stable European operations, supports a valuation materially above the current market price.

---

## Valuation Methodologies

### 1. Sum-of-the-Parts (SOTP)

The company was separated into its major operating segments:

- Germany
- Europe
- T-Systems
- T-Mobile US Stake

Each operating segment was valued independently using a discounted cash flow (DCF) model, while the TMUS stake was valued separately based on market value.

The individual segment values were then aggregated to derive Deutsche Telekom's implied equity value and target share price.

---

### 2. Trading Comparable Companies

A peer group of European telecommunications companies was selected:

- Orange SA
- Vodafone Group
- Telefónica SA
- KPN NV
- Swisscom AG

Valuation multiples analyzed:

- EV / Revenue
- EV / EBITDA
- EV / EBIT

A key adjustment was made to isolate Deutsche Telekom's European operations from its TMUS stake in order to create a like-for-like comparison with European telecom peers.

---

### 3. Precedent Transactions

Recent European telecommunications transactions were analyzed to estimate an implied control value for Deutsche Telekom.

Transaction multiples included:

- EV / Revenue
- EV / EBITDA
- EV / EBIT

This methodology provides an estimate of value under a potential acquisition scenario and incorporates a control premium relative to public market valuations.

---

### 4. Football Field Valuation

A football field analysis was constructed to compare valuation ranges implied by:

- Trading Comps
- SOTP Valuation
- Precedent Transactions

This provides a visual summary of the valuation outputs generated across methodologies.
